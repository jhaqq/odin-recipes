Holy Basil Chicken

(Pad Krapow Gai)



  This classic Thai dish was my favorite back in college. There was a restaurant a short walk from campus that got me absolutely hooked on the stuff. Holy Basil Chicken is a spicy, flavorful dish that is served over rice. It packs a ton of savory heat and I'm sure you'll love it as much as me if you give it a try!

  Serves: 2
  Prep time: 25 minutes

    Ingredients:
  • 1 lb chicken breast cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 4 cloves garlic minced
  • 2 tbsps Thai chilies (Serranos are a good sub!)
  • 1/4 cup shallots, sliced
  • 2 tbsp oil for frying
  • 1 cup thinly sliced basil
    • For the sauce, you'll need:
  • 1/3 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp each white and brown sugar
    Steps:
  1. Combine sauce ingredients in a bowl until homogenous.
  2. Preheat pan on high heat and add oil. Once to temp, add chicken and cook until done
    (2-3 min).
  3. Once cooked, add a tablespoon of sauce and cook for another minute.
  4. Pour the rest of the sauce and cook for another minute.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in the basil.
  6. Optional (but highly recommended!): Fry one egg and add to the top of the dish.

  This is my favorite recipe of all time. It is super spicy, so if you have a hard time with heat, I would opt for some less-spicy peppers. Thanks to AllRecipes for the great recipe.
